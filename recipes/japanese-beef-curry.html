Japanese Beef Curry

Description

Japanese curry, in contrast to other neighbouring types of curry, tends to be much lighter in terms of spices as well as thicker in terms of texture. It can even be surprisingly sweet, which makes it perfect for kids and the whole family. This recipe uses a premade dried curry roux, which can be bought at most Asian grocery stores, making the cooking process very quick and easy. With just a few simple ingredients, one could start and finish cooking within the span of an hour.

Ingredients

  • 1lb Beef, cut into bite-sized cubes
  • 1 Onions (Yellow)
  • 1 Large Potato
  • 1 Large Carrot
  • 1 clove Garlic
  • 1/2 tsp Ginger
  • 1/2 box Japanese Curry Roux
  • Water - see packaging of roux for amount
  • Salt and Pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 Apple (optional)
  • Rice, to serve

Steps

  1. Cut onions into wedges and sautee for 5 minutes, or until they turn lightly brown
  2. While onions are cooking, prepare the other vegetables: peel and cut potatoes and carrot into bite-sized pieces, mince garlic, grate ginger and apple
  3. Season and sear beef in large pot, if necessary cook multiple batches and don‘t overcrowd
  4. Add onions, and all vegetables to pot, pour in sufficient water
  5. Bring to boil, let simmer for around 30 minutes
  6. Turn off heat (the roux tends to burn easily) and add Roux cubes, stirring gently, until sauce is thickened
  7. Reheat and simmer for around 5 more minutes
  8. Serve, with rice
